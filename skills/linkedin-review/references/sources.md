## Comments vs likes weighting

- **Van der Blom 2025 Algorithm Insights Report** (1.8M post sample) — the most-cited current study. Substantive comments (15+ words) and back-and-forth threads with 3+ participants amplify reach ~5×. LinkedIn has not published an exact multiplier. [Announcement post](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/richardvanderblom_chapter-1-algorithm-insights-report-2025-activity-7322514599126130688-Q895) · [Lone Recruiter PDF mirror](https://thelonerecruiter.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Mastering-the-LinkedIn-Algorithm-in-202526-.pdf)
- **AuthoredUp synthesis** (citing Van der Blom) — [Algorithm guide](https://authoredup.com/blog/linkedin-algorithm)
- Don't quote "comments are 15× weighted than likes" as fact — that multiplier has no LinkedIn or Van der Blom source.

## Recruiter Spotlights (the real "active candidate" mechanic)

- **LinkedIn Recruiter Help — Spotlights** — [Spotlights documentation](https://www.linkedin.com/help/recruiter/answer/a414283). Real Spotlight names: *Open to Work, Active Talent, More Likely to Respond, Past Applicants, Company Followers, Internal Candidates, Rediscovered Candidates*.
- *Internal Candidates* Spotlight has the only published time threshold (≥6 months in current role).
- Spotlight-featured candidates: 64% higher InMail responses, 2× more likely to reply — [LinkedIn Talent Blog](https://www.linkedin.com/business/talent/blog/product-tips/spotlight-candidates-on-linkedin-who-are-open-to-new-opportunities) · [Onrec coverage](https://www.onrec.com/news/news-archive/linkedin%E2%80%99s-spotlight-feature-doubles-candidate-response-rate)
- **There is no documented "active in last 6 months" filter.** That number is folklore. The mechanic is Spotlight surfacing of recently-active candidates with no public day threshold.

## InMail economics

- LinkedIn-published average InMail response rate: 18–25% (3× cold email) — [LinkedIn Talent Blog](https://www.linkedin.com/business/talent/blog/talent-engagement/how-inmail-response-rates-compare-across-industries-and-functions)
- Recruiter quota mechanic: ≥13% reply rate over 14 days on 100+ messages or risk credit refund/throttling — [LinkedIn Recruiter Help](https://www.linkedin.com/help/recruiter/answer/a413271)
- 65% of replies arrive within 24h, 90% within a week — [LinkedIn Recruiter Help](https://www.linkedin.com/help/recruiter/answer/a414226)
- Personalized vs bulk: 40–44% lift in acceptance — [LinkedIn Talent Blog](https://www.linkedin.com/business/talent/blog/talent-strategy/these-inmails-get-best-response-rates)
- 2026 buyer-reported per-credit cost: ~$10; ~$900–$3,000 in credits per placement — [GLOZO 2026](https://www.glozo.com/blog/linkedin-inmail-cost-response-rate-math) · [Leonar 2026](https://www.leonar.app/blog/linkedin-inmail-cost/)

## What we deliberately removed (folklore)

These claims circulate widely but lack a citable LinkedIn or rigorous third-party source. The skill no longer makes them:

| Removed claim | Why |
|---|---|
| "Comments are 15× more weighted than likes" | No LinkedIn or Van der Blom source. Van der Blom's data is about thread depth (~5×), not a likes multiplier. |
| "All-Star profiles are 27× more discoverable" | Sourced to a 2021 LinkedIn Pulse user post, not LinkedIn itself. The "40× more opportunities" version is widely repeated but not on a current LinkedIn-owned page. |
| "80% of recruiters filter for active in last 6 months" | No LinkedIn documentation. Real mechanic is the Spotlight system. |
| "61 seconds dwell = 13× more reach" | "13× engagement rate" in third-party studies, not reach, not LinkedIn-published. |
| "Skill verification badges directly boost search ranking" | Skill Assessments were discontinued in 2024 and the badges purged. The replacement is credential-anchored Verified Skills via Entra. |
| "External links cause 60% reach penalty" | LinkedIn's Sr. Director publicly denied an intentional penalty in Aug 2025. Third-party data shows 25–50% reduction. Frame as tension, not certainty. |
