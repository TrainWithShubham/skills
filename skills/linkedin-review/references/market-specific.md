# Market-Specific LinkedIn Advice

LinkedIn norms differ across markets. This is especially important because resume conventions (no photo, no DOB, etc. in international resumes) DO NOT apply to LinkedIn — LinkedIn is a social platform where photos and visual profiles are expected.

---

## India Market

### Use the new India-only fields (Oct 2025)

This is the single most important update for India profiles. In **October 2025**, LinkedIn shipped India-exclusive fields under the Open to Work setting, **visible only to recruiters**:

- **Notice Period / Availability to Join**
- **Expected Annual Salary**

Built specifically for the Indian IT-services market where 60–90 day notice periods are standard. Use these official fields rather than:
- Stuffing "Serving Notice Period" into the headline (wastes the highest-weighted ranking field)
- Putting CTC in About or Featured (the field is recruiter-only — keeps it out of public view)

If the user is in India and these fields are not filled, flag it as a high-leverage 2-minute fix.

### What's different from a resume

- **Photo is essentially mandatory.** Indian recruiters treat a missing photo as a credibility flag. Profiles with photos see 14× more views in India-specific data.
- **Naukri-LinkedIn parallel matters.** Indian tech recruiters operate multi-platform — TCS/Infosys/Wipro rely heavily on Naukri Resdex; product companies use LinkedIn + Hirist + iimjobs + Cutshort. **Recruiters cross-reference.** Keep dates, titles, companies, skills, and notice period in sync across LinkedIn ↔ Naukri ↔ resume.
- **Open to Work is widely used in India and not stigmatized** — especially post-2023 layoffs. The public green ring works well at junior/mid level. Recruiter-only mode is still safer for senior or status-sensitive roles.
- **Bengaluru, not Bangalore.** LinkedIn's canonical tag is "Bengaluru, Karnataka, India" — recruiter Boolean searches in 2025–2026 favor Bengaluru.
- **No CTC publicly.** Use the new recruiter-only Expected Annual Salary field. Never in About, Headline, or Featured.

### India is LinkedIn's lead market for verification

Per TechCrunch (Dec 2025), LinkedIn's profile-verification push (Verified workplace via Microsoft Entra, Verified ID, work-email verification) is rolling out fastest in India. **Verified Indian profiles outperform unverified.** If the user has a corporate work email or their employer is on Microsoft Entra, completing workplace verification is a 5-minute high-leverage step.

### Indian-specific content patterns that work

- **Tech-conference speaking:** DevOps India Summit, KubeCon India, AWS reInvent India, Rootconf — pin to Featured at senior level.
- **Service → Product transitions** need explicit reframing. Strip client/project names from headline; replace with product-thinking keywords (SLI/SLO, error budgets, autoscaling, on-call, blast-radius). Add GitHub link in Contact Info and Featured — counters the DSA (L1) and System Design (3–5 yr) ceilings that product companies screen on.
- **Naukri JobSpeak Index** is the most-cited Indian hiring barometer. Referencing recent JobSpeak data in a post boosts recruiter engagement.

### India-specific red flags

- **"Ex-TCS / Ex-Infosys / Ex-Wipro" in headline** — wastes keyword space and leads with a past employer instead of expertise. Keep in Experience only.
- **"Serving Notice Period" in headline** — outdated workaround now that the official field exists.
- **Hobbies / personal details in About** ("loves cricket, travel, food") — delete; saves space for signal.
- **Father's name / marital status / DOB** — legacy Indian resume template leaking into LinkedIn. Remove.
- **Public CTC disclosure** in About / Featured / Headline ("My current CTC is 22 LPA, expected 35 LPA") — strictly recruiter-only field territory now (see Oct 2025 Expected Annual Salary field). Public disclosure reads as desperation, signals weak negotiation, and locks in a number you may want to revise upward. Move to the recruiter-only field; remove from About.

### Title inflation detection (India-specific)

Indian fintech, service-company, and many startup orgs inflate titles by **1–2 levels** vs international product-company norms. A "Senior DevOps Engineer" at a 4-year mark in an Indian fintech ≈ mid-level DevOps Engineer at Razorpay/Flipkart/Stripe-tier. A "Lead" at a service company ≈ mid+ at a product company. An "Architect" title before 8 years is almost always service-company convention.

**How to handle when reviewing:**
- Detect from years-of-experience vs current title. Common inflation patterns:
  - "Senior" with <5 years at TCS/Infosys/Wipro/Cognizant/Mindtree/HCL/fintech-startup-tier → recalibrate as mid-level for target-market scoring.
  - "Lead" with <7 years → mid+ for product-company target.
  - "Architect" / "Principal" with <8 years → senior IC for product-company target.
- Be candid in the review: name the title-inflation pattern as a positioning risk, not a personal flaw. *"'Senior' at PayBolt at 4 years won't be honored at Razorpay/Flipkart-tier — they screen for product-company seniority equivalents. Drop 'Senior' from the headline rewrite for the target market; let the scope and outcomes do the work."*
- For the **headline rewrite for international or product-company targets**, drop the inflated title and lead with role + scope instead. E.g., `DevOps Engineer | AWS · EKS · Terraform | Owned 25-microservice infra at PayBolt fintech | Cut deploy time 40%` beats `Senior DevOps Engineer | …` for a Razorpay application — the second framing invites scrutiny the first doesn't.
- For the **current employer / India recruiters**, keeping "Senior" is fine (it matches the org's convention). The conflict only emerges when targeting up-market.

### Campus hiring / freshers (India)

- **Projects section** is critical at fresher level — most freshers ignore it.
- **Featured section** with 2–3 GitHub repos with strong READMEs carries heavy weight.
- **Certifications** — AWS Cloud Practitioner, Terraform Associate, CKA, Docker Certified — sync into Licenses & Certifications and tag skills to them (the modern Verified Skills mechanic that replaced the dead Skill Assessment badges).
- **"Aspiring DevOps Engineer / Open to Work / Recent Graduate"** in headline — wastes the heaviest-weighted field. Lead with the target role: `DevOps Engineer | AWS · Docker · Jenkins | Built 3 CI/CD pipelines · AWS Cloud Practitioner`.

### Reverse migration context (2025–2026)

After the US H-1B fee hike, LinkedIn data shows a **40% YoY rise in tech professionals moving to India** (Q3 2025). FAANG India headcount is up ~18% YoY (32K+ jobs). This affects positioning:

- For Indians targeting senior FAANG India roles: emphasize **product-scale metrics** (req/s, p99, MTTR), not enterprise-IT vocabulary.
- For Indians targeting US/UK roles: with H-1B at $100K, default to **multi-geography signaling** (UK Skilled Worker, Germany Blue Card, Ireland Critical Skills) rather than US-only. Place visa flexibility in About: `Open to UK / EU / US relocation; have completed [whatever you've done]`.

## International / Remote Market

### What's different from a resume

- **Photo is fine on LinkedIn** (unlike international resumes, where it's a red flag in some markets due to anti-bias norms). LinkedIn first-party data: 21× more views, 9× more connection requests with a photo.
- **Timezone signal** in About or banner, especially for remote roles targeting specific regions.

### What's required for international/remote success

- **English-first writing.** Zero grammatical errors. Remote-first companies filter aggressively on communication quality. About-section voice is the unstated English screen.
- **Timezone in About:** e.g., "Based in Bengaluru / IST (UTC+5:30), 4-hour overlap with US PST / full overlap with CET."
- **Visa / work authorization, if relevant:** "EU passport holder" / "No US visa sponsorship required" saves recruiter time. If you need sponsorship, state clearly — international recruiters filter India-located profiles out for timezone reasons unless explicitly flagged.
- **GitHub profile prominently linked.** US/EU companies cross-check heavily — ~30% of senior software engineers don't have an active LinkedIn at all, so GitHub is often weighted equally.
- **English content creation** — blog posts on Hashnode/Medium/personal site, conference talks, newsletters — amplifies international credibility.

### International-specific levers

- **Long-form English content scales.** A single well-written LinkedIn article can land 3–5 recruiter DMs from US/EU companies.
- **Participate in #DevOps / #SRE / #PlatformEngineering threads.** The international DevOps community is active on LinkedIn — substantive comments on posts by Kelsey Hightower, Charity Majors, Jason Yee, etc. surface your name.
- **Conference talks > certifications** at senior level. KubeCon, SREcon, DevOpsDays, PromCon — pin to Featured.

### International-specific red flags

- **Public Open-to-Work green ring on a senior profile** — often reads as desperation in US/EU senior hiring. Use **recruiter-only mode** instead — LinkedIn's first-party data shows 14.5% positive InMail response with the recruiter-only signal vs 4.6% without (~3× lift).
- **Generic "Seeking opportunities in [Country]"** — low signal. Better: "Open to remote Senior DevOps roles with async-first teams."
- **Missing timezone info** for remote targets — recruiters filter on this.
- **Indian-only metrics without context** ("served 10 Cr users") — translate to international units (100M users).

### Remote-ready signals to highlight

- Async communication practices (documented decisions, written specs)
- Documentation-driven culture
- Self-managed work patterns
- Experience with Slack / Notion / Linear / async-first tooling
- Async DevOps practices (runbooks, post-mortems as docs, not meetings)

### Companies that genuinely hire remotely from India

Crossover, Turing, Toptal, GitLab, Automattic, Zapier, HashiCorp, MongoDB, Stripe (some roles), Cloudflare (some roles). Worth tagging if applying.

## When the market is unclear

If the profile and JD don't clearly signal India or International, ask:

> "Are you targeting India-based roles, international/remote, or both? The advice differs on a few things — timezone signals, Open-to-Work mode, the new India-only Notice Period field, and how to frame your current role."

If they're targeting both:
- Maintain one LinkedIn but optimize for international rules (stricter English, timezone callout)
- India-specific things (Bengaluru location, the Oct-2025 Notice Period field) don't hurt for international targets and DO help for India ones
- Skip the public Open-to-Work green ring; use **recruiter-only** mode — it serves Indian recruiters and avoids the international "desperation" read

## How market affects scoring

| Dimension | India bias | International bias |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Completeness | Photo is essentially mandatory; verification rollout is fastest in India | Photo expected (unlike resumes); workplace verification still high-value |
| Discoverability | Naukri-parallel keywords matter; Bengaluru not Bangalore | GitHub + content creation weighted higher |
| Headline & About | Fill the official Notice Period + Expected CTC fields, not the headline; timezone if targeting outside India | Timezone + visa status mandatory for remote |
| Experience | Scale metrics in lakhs/Cr OK for India-only roles | Translate to international units (100M, $400K) |
| Social Proof | Recommendations from Indian engineers count fully; verification badge weighted | Recommendations from international companies/managers weighted more |
