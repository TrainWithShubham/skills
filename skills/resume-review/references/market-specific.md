# Market-Specific Advice

Resume norms differ by market. Apply the right set of expectations based on the target.

---

## India market

Use these rules when the resume + JD indicate an India-focused role.

### What to include

- **Photo** — acceptable in India, expected by some (especially consulting, BFSI). Pass-through — no deduction.
- **Naukri profile** — matters as much as the resume. Naukri has its own ranking algorithm; updating the profile every 48 hours moves candidates up in recruiter searches. Mirror resume keywords in Naukri headline and key skills.
- **LinkedIn URL** — always.
- **Location** — city, state (e.g., "Bengaluru, Karnataka"). State is useful because many recruiters filter by state.
- **Notice period** — if less than 30 days or currently serving, mention it subtly in the summary (e.g., "Immediate joiner" or "30-day notice"). This is a hiring advantage in India.

### What to skip

- **CTC (current compensation)** — never put CTC on the resume. If forms ask, give a range. Research market rate on levels.fyi, Glassdoor, AmbitionBox.
- **Expected CTC** — same. Leave for negotiation.

### Service → Product transition

Many Indian engineers are transitioning from services (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant) to product companies. Frame service work in product terms:
- Don't say "client project" — name the domain, the scale, the impact.
- Don't say "deployment support" — describe what you actually built or decided.
- Emphasize **ownership** over **task execution**.

### Campus hiring / freshers (India)

- College tier matters less than projects + certifications + GitHub activity.
- Show you can **do** things, not just **study** them.
- Certifications that move the needle for freshers: AWS Cloud Practitioner, CKA/CKAD, Terraform Associate.
- List internships before education if they're 3+ months and relevant.

### India-specific red flags in reviews

Flag these when you see them:
- "Expected CTC: X LPA" on the resume — remove.
- Page with DOB, blood group, father's name, religion, marital status — remove (legacy Indian template).
- "Curriculum Vitae" as the title of the document — remove.
- Hobbies section with "reading books, listening to music, making friends" — remove (no signal).

## International / Remote market

Use these rules when the resume + JD indicate an international or remote role.

### What to include

- **Timezone signal** — e.g., "IST (UTC+5:30) | 4-hour overlap with US EST" in the header. Remote companies need to know timezone compatibility before they read anything else.
- **GitHub URL** — non-negotiable. Active contributions > star count. Open source PRs, well-documented repos, clean READMEs carry huge weight.
- **Portfolio / blog link** — if the candidate has technical writing or content, link it.
- **English-first writing** — zero grammatical errors. Get a native speaker or AI to proofread. This is non-negotiable for remote roles.
- **Remote-ready skills** — mention async communication, documentation-driven culture, tools like Slack/Notion/Linear, self-managed work patterns. These are valued signals for remote roles.
- **Visa status (if relevant)** — e.g., "US work authorization: not required" or "EU passport holder". Clarifies filtering upfront.

### What to skip

- **Photo** — no. Anti-discrimination laws in US, UK, Canada, EU, Australia make photos a red flag for recruiters. Some will not process applications with photos.
- **DOB, age, gender, marital status** — same reason.
- **Full street address** — just city, country.
- **Certifications that are only recognized in India** (e.g., unknown regional bootcamps) — don't list them for international roles.

### Impact at scale

International companies respond to scale stories. "Managed infra for 1M+ users" hits differently than "managed servers". Push scale metrics into the summary if the candidate has them.

### ATS is critical for international

US/UK/EU companies use modern ATS pipelines heavily. Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby — all keyword-driven. ATS optimization is more important for international applications than it is for typical Indian recruitment agencies (which often work off LinkedIn or Naukri profiles directly).

### Remote-specific red flags

Flag these:
- No timezone indicator.
- Generic summary with no scale or impact numbers.
- Typos or grammar errors.
- GitHub URL missing or points to an empty/stale profile.
- Listing "Fluent in English" as a skill (this is assumed — listing it is a soft negative signal).

## When the market is unclear

If the resume and JD don't clearly signal India or International:

1. **Ask the user directly**: "Is this for roles in India, international/remote, or both?"
2. If they're targeting both, note in the review that advice will cover both, and flag items that should differ between the two versions of the resume (e.g., include photo for India version, remove for international version).

---

## Adjusting the review

Once you know the market, let it influence:

- **Dimension 5 (Contact & Presence)** — photo deduction only applies to international; LinkedIn customization matters more for India; GitHub matters universally.
- **Dimension 4 (Structure)** — no change based on market.
- **Dimension 2 (Keywords)** — international JDs often want full forms ("Amazon Web Services" not "AWS"); Indian JDs are more forgiving.
- **Priority Fixes** — market-specific issues (e.g., "add timezone signal for remote") show up here.
